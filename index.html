Graphics & Motion Design


CLARK DENTAL

X

During my time working at FMC I was in charge of create a new advertising campaign for one of our main clients Clark Dental. The brief was to replace all of the existing print and digital adverts with some their latest range of products in a minimalist/clinical. layout. The primary products they wanted to promote was a range of dental chairs and cabinetry. I was required to create realistic compositions with the photos adding the light and shadow as well as getting the scale correct. The final outcomes were to be used mainly in print publishing but these were resized and formatted for social media and other digital advertising.

SOFTWARE: PHOTOSHOP | ILLUSTRATOR | INDESIGN


HARPER'S TRILOGY

X

I was given a breief in my role at FMC to create a series of book covers for a trilogy of romance novels by one of our clients. The concept was for the book to appear almost like a gift box with the ribbon wrapped around the front and back covers and onto the spine as well as including a texture for the background as well using spot colour printing to give a raised surface to ribbon giving the overall design that extra dimension enhancing the overall final outcome. STEAMPUNK TYPEFACE

X

This typeface was created as part of my end of year exhibition in my third year of university. The brief was to take an aspect from all of the projects that had been worked on for that year and create a show piece for the exhibition. What I did was take what was originally only a few letters that had been created for an art poster. I then used a standard sans-serif font and made my own guidelines and then added the extra elements for the aesthetics. The final output was an original typeface design with a steampunk/retro futurist style. The display was a single A0 glossy UV print with a portrait orientation which was suspended on wire at the exhibition.

SOFTWARE: ILLUSTRATOR | PHOTOSHOP

MARITIME LONDON

X

I was tasked while working at Elaborate with putting together a series of testimonial videos to be distributed among the Maritime London corporate membership. I needed to keep the layout simple and within some basic guidelines. Other things to consider would be the intro/outro so the whole video doesn't begin and end abruptly, background and intro music, fonts and some visual references representing London. A specific requirement of the contributors was to keep the testimonials within a few minutes and interlinked with short transition clips inbetween to give more variation. The final outcome is a clean/corporate short film which despite a lot of different elements, contributions and liasing with different people it was quite a straightforward project which came together quite well.

The Ship Supplier

X

I was tasked with rebuilding the whole website for The Ship Supplier from the ground up. The old site had become dated with broken components and needed a full refresh. The design had to be kept simple and user friendly and aimed at a corporate audience. I also had to keep a few things in mind when designing the UI. The colour scheme needed to be simple to match the branding colour scheme, a simple UI almost like a news site with some extra compents to also modernise it and also to add some to functionality for usability. This included making it mobile responsive, adding a contact form, connecting it to social media channels and adding a digital flipbook for the digital magazines all of which didn't exist in the previouse website

The final outcome both fixes all of these issues as well as giving the website a new clean aesthetic adding new elements like home page videos and transition in image galleries to help set the design off to prevent it looking plain.

VISIT THE LIVE WEBSITE WWW.THESHIPSUPPLIER.COM

SOFTWARE: HTML | CSS | JAVASCRIPT | VISUAL STUDIO CODE | PHOTOSHOP | AFTER EFFECTS
